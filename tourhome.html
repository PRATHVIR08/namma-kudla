About Mangalore

Mangalore, locally known as Kudla, is a vibrant coastal city nestled between the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats in Karnataka. With a rich history dating back over a thousand years, it has served as an important port for trade with Persia and Arabia. Mangalore’s culture reflects a harmonious blend of Tulunadu traditions, Konkani influences, and colonial heritage.

The city is dotted with ancient temples, pristine beaches, and bustling markets. Its architecture displays influences from Portuguese and Vijayanagara eras. Known for its hospitality and scenic beauty, Mangalore attracts travelers who wish to experience authentic coastal life — from fishing harbors to cultural festivals like Dasara and Kambala. The local people, fluent in Tulu, Konkani, and Kannada, are known for their warmth and strong community values.

Special Cuisine of Kudla

Mangalore’s cuisine is a flavorful celebration of the coast. Coconut, curry leaves, tamarind, and freshly caught seafood dominate the kitchen. Popular dishes include *Neer Dosa, **Chicken Ghee Roast, **Bangude Pulimunchi (Mackerel Curry)*, and *Kori Rotti* — a spicy chicken curry paired with crispy rice wafers.

Vegetarian delights like Pathrode, Sanna, and Bajil Uppit reflect the traditional Tulu and Konkani households. Sweet lovers can’t miss Mangalore Buns and Payasam. The aroma of filter coffee and the tang of solkadhi perfectly complete a Kudla meal. For food enthusiasts, every bite in Mangalore tells a story of spice, sea, and tradition.
